STF - Supreme Federal Court

Supreme Federal Court (STF) is the highest instance of the Brazilian judiciary and accumulates both the typical powers of a supreme court, that is, a court of last resort, as well as a constitutional court, which would be the one that judges issues of constitutionality regardless of concrete disputes. Its fundamental institutional function is to serve as guardian of the 1988 Federal Constitution, appreciating cases that involve injury or threat to the latter. There is no appeal to any other court against its decisions.
